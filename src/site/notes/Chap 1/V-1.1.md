### ğŸ•¶ï¸ The Blind King Within  [[Dhritarashtra\|Dhritarashtra]]

Although Dhritarashtra does not have any physical blindness, he has not one ounce of conscious perception. While Dhritarashtra can see the palace he lives in, the thousands of subjects under him, and the heights to which he has a positioned himself, he still cannot perceive the things that motivate him towards all of the above; that being his own greed and attachment.

This implies that **we** are all blinded to some degree because we live out of unconsciousness(Same as what Mahavira called it as Murcha) or by following the habits we create through conditioning.

The way in which we believe we see is created through our conditioning, or the programming that we have received from family and society.

### ğŸ‘ï¸ Sanjaya The Watcher Within

Sanjaya represents the "seer" who sees across distances as a "testimony" or "eye-witness." In the Indian spiritual traditions, the term for this form of seeing is **à¤¸à¤¾à¤•à¥à¤·à¥€ à¤­à¤¾à¤µ (sakshi bhav)** or the "witness consciousness". It reflects the awareness we possess when observing the world outside of our thoughts or emotions without any attachment to what we witness.

When Dhritarashtra experienced blindness, he nonetheless had a desire to experience an understanding of the world outside of his immediate perception. Dhritarashtra found clarity by using Sanjaya as a channel to connect with this knowledge. The internal flame that dwells within Dhritarashtra allowed him to observe himself and connect with his knowledge. Sanjaya was the vehicle through which Dhritarashtra connected to the truth of what was taking place around him - all the events and actions taking place on the battlefield were reported to him by Sanjaya.

Similarly, when we take a moment and stop to ponder over the thoughts that race through our minds, we can see the world through the eyes of Sanjaya. We do not have to identify ourselves with our thoughts and beliefs, nor do we need to act on what our emotions compel us to do. Instead, we simply become aware of what happens around us through our own perception, with no need for external validation.
